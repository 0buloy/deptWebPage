ABOUT US

The Computer Society is an esteemed organization within the Polytechnic University of the Philippines (PUP) in the Philippines. It is a dynamic and influential student-led group that is dedicated to the advancement and promotion of computer science and technology-related fields within the university and its surrounding community.

Mission

In consonance with the PUP Vision, the PUP GS commits itself to foster excellence in all key dimensions of graduate education: instruction, research, extension, community service, production, industry-academe partnerships, local and international linkages, and stakeholders empowerment.

PUP: The National Polytechnic University

Comsoc | History

The first batch of Bachelor of Information Technology was admitted in 2006. Prior to this batch, the PUP Sto. Tomas branch offered Bachelor of Computer and Data Processing Management in 1992. It was later changed to the BS Computer Science program in 2000 and then to BSIT program.

The BSIT Program was offered to address the current needs and trends in the job market. In 2010, sixty-three pioneer BSIT students attended their graduation. To date, there are already 12 batches of BSIT successful graduates. According to the Graduate Tracer Study, employability of BSIT Graduates range from 91 to 96% which indicate that PUPSTB BSIT graduates are in-demand by the companies here and abroad.

Over the years, ComSoc has been instrumental in fostering a strong community of technology enthusiasts, organizing various technical and non-technical activities, and promoting digital literacy within the Polytechnic University of the Philippines and its surrounding community.
